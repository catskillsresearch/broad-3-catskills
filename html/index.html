Solution Overview

Our Catskills Solution for Crunch 3 of the Broad Institute IBD Challenge focuses on identifying gene markers that distinguish dysplastic (pre-cancerous) from non-dysplastic tissue regions in inflammatory bowel disease samples. We've developed a comprehensive approach that integrates techniques from previous crunches while adding specific innovations for Crunch 3.

DeepSpot (Crunch 1)

Leverages multi-level spatial context integration and deep-set neural networks to predict measured gene expression from H&E images.

Tarandros (Crunch 2)

Employs cell-wise optimization and similarity-based methods to infer unmeasured gene expression with superior cell-wise Spearman correlation.

LogFC Method (Crunch 3)

Applies log fold change analysis to rank genes based on their ability to differentiate between dysplastic and non-dysplastic regions.

Unified Approach

Combines the strengths of all three approaches into a single, integrated solution with balanced gene-wise and cell-wise optimization.

Streamlined Pipeline

Our solution features a streamlined pipeline that removes unused data elements and clearly shows the transformations from input data to final gene rankings. The pipeline is implemented using Luigi, which manages dependencies between tasks and ensures that each step is executed in the correct order.

We've organized the pipeline to support three different dataset approaches: small real dataset for rapid experimentation, synthetic dataset for controlled validation, and large real dataset for final training and evaluation.

Integrated Techniques

Our solution integrates techniques from all three crunches to effectively identify gene markers that distinguish dysplastic from non-dysplastic tissue regions:

  • DeepSpot (Crunch 1): Multi-level spatial context integration, deep-set neural networks, and sophisticated attention mechanisms
  • Tarandros (Crunch 2): Cell-wise optimization, similarity-based gene inference, and reference dataset integration
  • LogFC Method (Crunch 3): Log fold change analysis, statistical significance testing, and gene ranking

Multi-Dataset Approach

Our solution leverages three different dataset types for comprehensive model development and evaluation:

  • Small Real Dataset: For rapid development, testing, and debugging
  • Synthetic Dataset: For controlled experiments, validation, and hyperparameter tuning
  • Large Real Dataset: For final model training, evaluation, and biological insights

This multi-dataset approach allows for efficient development, rigorous validation, and robust final results.
